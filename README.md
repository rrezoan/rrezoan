## 👤 About me

I'm an experimental materials scientist with PhD-level training in thermoelectric materials, phonon transport, and advanced characterisation. My work sits at the interface of fundamental physics and applied engineering: I design and synthesise materials, characterise them across structural, thermal, electrical, magnetic, and mechanical properties, and use neutron scattering and DFT-informed analysis to explain why a material behaves the way it does.

What I bring to a team is the ability to connect laboratory evidence to engineering decisions — troubleshooting experiments, improving repeatability, documenting workflows, and translating complex results for academic, industry, and partner-facility stakeholders. I have hands-on experience spanning university research labs (ISEM, UOW), national facilities (ANSTO, CSIRO), and a live, government-funded industry process project (recycled PET filament with Deco Australia).

Open to roles in: Materials R&D · Advanced characterisation · Postdoctoral research · Process / Quality / Manufacturing engineering — across Australia.

## 🚀 Projects

### 1. Carbon-Modified Mg₂Si Thermoelectric Materials (PhD core project)

**Goal:** Reduce the lattice thermal conductivity of Mg₂Si thermoelectrics through carbon-induced interfaces and defects, without destroying electrical performance.

**What I did:** Carbon / Bi co-doping and synthesis, followed by a full characterisation chain — XRD → SEM/EDS → TEM → thermal & electrical transport → neutron spectroscopy → DFT-informed interpretation.

**Solved / achieved:** Built an evidence-based picture linking carbon-induced defects to phonon scattering and reduced thermal transport; results form the backbone of my PhD thesis and two manuscripts in preparation.

**Learned:** How to design a multi-technique experimental campaign where each method validates the next, and how to reconcile structural, transport, and scattering data into a single mechanism.

---

### 2. Neutron Scattering & Phonon Engineering

**Goal:** Understand how different carbon allotropes and Mg₂Si systems vibrate, and how that vibrational behaviour controls heat flow.

**What I did:** Inelastic neutron scattering campaigns at ANSTO; interpreted vibrational/phonon density of states (VDOS/GDOS) using LAMP workflows; compared microdiamond, few-layer graphene, and amorphous carbon fibres.

**Solved / achieved:** First-author publication in Applied Physics Letters (2026) comparing phonons and vibrations across carbon allotropes; contributed to identifying the propagon-to-diffuson thermal-transport crossover in SnTe (J. Appl. Phys. 2025).

**Learned:** End-to-end neutron experiment workflow — proposal-to-publication — and how to connect spectroscopic signatures to macroscopic thermal properties.

---

### 3. Polymer, Recycled Plastic & Composite Filament Development

**Goal:** Turn post-use PET and composite feedstocks into reliable, recyclable filaments for thermal management and 3D printing.

**What I did:** As Lead Engineer on the Deco Australia × UOW (NSW Government-funded) project, developed and optimised an extrusion process — controlling feedstock consistency, dispersion, and extrusion parameters; characterised output with Micro-CT and thermal analysis.

**Solved / achieved:** Established a repeatable process producing strong, consistent results to date; delivered progress against an industry + government program with reporting to external stakeholders.

**Learned:** Real-world process and quality engineering — moving from lab-scale insight to a controlled, repeatable manufacturing workflow with industry accountability.

---

### 4. Magnetic Polymer Nanocomposites (Fe₃O₄ / Polyurethane)

**Goal:** Understand and exploit the coupling between thermal and magnetic behaviour in superparamagnetic polymer composites.

**What I did:** Synthesised Fe₃O₄/polymer systems; characterised with VSM magnetometry, AC inductive (remote) heating, and thermal conductivity measurements.

**Solved / achieved:** First-author publication in Journal of Magnetism and Magnetic Materials (2023) mapping the interplay between thermal and magnetic properties — relevant to remote-heating and smart-material applications.

**Learned:** How to design composites where two property domains (thermal and magnetic) interact, and how to measure that coupling rigorously.

---

### 5. Flexible Thermoelectric & Wearable Composite Prototypes

**Goal:** Translate thermoelectric and composite materials into manufacturable, flexible prototypes.

**What I did:** Hot pressing, lamination, and bonding of flexible composites with a focus on manufacturability.

**Solved / achieved:** Demonstrated prototype routes that bridge materials performance and practical fabrication constraints.

**Learned:** Design-for-manufacture thinking — balancing material performance against process feasibility.

---

### 6. Textile Engineering & Manufacturing Quality (foundations)

**Goal:** Link process parameters to product quality in a high-volume manufacturing environment.

**What I did:** Hands-on across spinning, knitting, weaving, dyeing, finishing and cut-and-sew; applied QA/QC methods including AQL, SPC, and 5S.

**Solved / achieved:** Built early, practical fluency in manufacturing quality systems that now underpins my process-engineering work.

**Learned:** How disciplined quality control and statistical process thinking keep production consistent — foundations I carry into materials manufacturing.
